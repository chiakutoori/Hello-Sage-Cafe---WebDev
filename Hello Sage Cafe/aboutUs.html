Our Story

Hello! Sage Cafe started as a longshot dream. I began as a home baker after 8 years of working a typical 8–5 job. My love for baking opened new doors of opportunities to meet people—many of whom helped me build the cafe I had always dreamt of.

My first platform was "Desserts by Drewwwlll" (@dessertsbydrewwwlll). I started by baking cookies and cheesecakes, selling them to my former coworkers, and to my brother's and sister's friends and colleagues. At that time, I was just beginning to understand the deeper art of baking. I was a one-woman team—doing the baking, packing, distributing, and sometimes even the delivering. It was a stark contrast to my old job of sitting at a desk all day. I was active, busy, and honestly, it felt really healthy for me! For three years, I managed everything—planning offerings, taking orders, baking, packing, and delivering. I became known for creating personalized cakes. But everything took a huge turn when I found out I was pregnant. At 30, I hadn't expected it, especially after being told by doctors that it would be difficult for us to conceive. So I had found peace with raising my furbabies (three dogs and sixteen cats) and growing my online baking business.
I thought being pregnant wouldn't stop what I was doing. I hoped it wouldn't. But the first trimester hit me hard. My body was doing things I didn't understand. Mornings, which I used to love, became the hardest part of my day. I wasn't able to bake for nine months—and even for nearly two years after that. Still, my love for baking never left. I remember writing my dreams down in a journal: my plans, my vision. When Sage was born, those dreams were put on pause, but I would often go back to that journal to remind myself what I wanted. I wanted to be a present mom, but I also wanted my dreams. I wanted Sage to grow up seeing her mom go after what she loves. And that's what I did—with the support of my husband, we made it happen. I reconnected with Pia, a classmate from baking school. She introduced me to her best friend Cy, a chef. Then we found Jhen, a barista. That's how our team was born. My brother-in-law, Kuya Khamer, whom I also consider a mentor, gave me the push I needed. He helped me visualize it and stood by me through the process.
Our first cafe was located inside Antrips Antipolo, an Airbnb owned by friends. It was a great place to start, though the location lacked foot traffic. Six months later, we moved to Hollywood Terraces. We didn't plan on targeting students, but it felt like a realignment—and we embraced the challenge. As Sage grew up, so did the cafe. She became close to our regulars and staff. She practically grew up in the cafe.
This May 23rd marks our 2nd anniversary. I now have a new team, as my original staff have moved on to their own paths. It was tough to transition after we had established a flow, but change also brings growth. Just like the many changes that came and went, we are entering a new era. My love for writing is returning. Sage is becoming more playful and sociable—she loves to scribble and doodle. So, I love the idea that while she enjoys herself, I can enjoy what I love, too. A friend of mine inspired me, saying that starting over again might just be what's needed. It's tough, but worth it—to give birth to new energy by integrating all the things I love.
My original vision for the cafe was to create a place where people could be themselves, express freely, and connect. Writing has always helped me process feelings I couldn't always post online. That's when I thought of having a "freedom wall"—a space where anyone can write anonymously. And I was amazed by how students expressed themselves. Some wrote about dreams, thoughts, even deeply personal things. People passing by would stop to read. I love how we can all resonate with each other. While not everything has unfolded yet, I am deeply grateful to everyone who visited the cafe—those who laughed with us, ordered food, enjoyed a drink, wrote, read, or simply sat in silence. You are all manifestations of a dream I once wrote in my journal. And every day, I'm amazed that I get to live it—alongside Sage, my greatest inspiration.

-Drew Ramos
